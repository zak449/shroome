# shroome Photography & Image Direction

> Last updated: March 2026
> Owner: Brand Director / Creative Lead

---

## Brand Energy

shroome is a **young energy brand**. Every image should feel like it belongs in a world where functional wellness meets culture — not a yoga retreat catalog. Think elevated convenience, not ceremony.

**The vibe:** Saturday morning, sunlight through the window, good music on, making your drink before you head out.

---

## Hard Rules

### Never Use
- Whisks, bamboo scoops, or matcha preparation tools
- Loose matcha powder on surfaces or in bowls — NO POWDERS EVER
- Traditional tea ceremony imagery
- Zen gardens, meditation poses, or "wellness" stock photography
- Mushrooms in their raw/whole form (no forest floor shots)
- AI-generated or AI-modified product images — the sachets are sacred
- Generic/fake sachets — ONLY use the real designed sachets (see below)
- Stock photography of generic supplements or pills
- Clinical / laboratory aesthetics
- Any cup/glass without "shroomé" branding — if a cup appears, it must say "shroomé"

### Sachet Design Reference (LOCKED — do not deviate)
- **Vanilla sachet:** Cream/off-white with dark green serif "shroomé" text, vanilla flower icon, "pour / swirl / glow" tagline, 1 fl oz | 30 mL
- **Strawberry sachet:** Soft pink (#FFB7D1) with dark green serif "shroomé" text, strawberry icon, same layout as vanilla
- Both sachets list: ceremonial matcha, grass-fed collagen, mushroom beta glucans
- Source files: `sachet-vanilla.png` and `sachet-strawberry.png` on drinkshroome.com
- NEVER create, illustrate, or AI-generate alternative sachet designs
- When sachets appear in lifestyle photography, they must match these exact designs or use the actual product image overlaid

### The Product Inside
- Each sachet contains **1 fl oz (30 mL) of dark green liquid concentrate**
- It is NOT a powder — it is a ready-to-pour liquid
- The "pour / swirl / glow" moment: dark green liquid pours into milk and swirls into a bright, creamy matcha latte
- This swirl moment is the hero visual for video content — capture the contrast of dark green hitting white milk
- Final drink appearance: bright green matcha latte with creamy foam
- Never show dry powder, scoops, or mixing — the product is liquid, pour and go

### Always Prioritize
- Real product photography — the sachet is the hero
- Energy and movement over stillness and calm
- Warmth and approachability over luxury minimalism
- People actually enjoying the drink, not posing with it

## Product Photography

### The Sachet is the Hero
- Every product shot must feature real, photographed sachets
- Source sachet images from actual product photography only
- Never recreate, illustrate, or digitally alter the sachet design
- The sachet is the most recognizable brand asset — protect it

### Starburst Lighting
- Product shots use a **starburst lighting approach** — bright, radiant light emanating from behind or around the product
- This creates a glow effect that makes the sachet feel premium and alive
- Light should feel natural and warm, not clinical or studio-harsh
- Shadows should be soft and minimal — the product floats in light

### Composition
- Clean negative space around the product — let it breathe
- Angled shots welcome (not always straight-on)
- Show the sachet at a scale where text/design is readable
- Group shots (multiple sachets) should feel casual, not rigid grid layouts

## Lifestyle Photography

### Tone
- Energetic, not hyperactive
- Warm, not sterile
- Real, not staged
- Young, not juvenile

### Settings
- Home kitchens (modern, lived-in, not showroom)
- Cafes and coffee shops
- Desks and workspaces (creative, not corporate)
- Outdoor mornings (natural light, warm tones)

### People
- Diverse, young-adult demographic (22-35)
- Candid over posed — caught mid-moment, not looking at camera
- Holding or making the drink, not just standing near it
- Expression: focused, smiling, in-flow — never forced

## Background & Texture Direction

### Cloud Imagery
- Use organic, dreamy cloud imagery for website and marketing backgrounds
- Clouds should feel soft, textural, and slightly surreal
- Color-tinted clouds preferred — lean into the brand palette (Lavender, Blush, Soft Lavender)
- Source from custom-generated imagery (ChatGPT images approved) for backgrounds only
- Clouds communicate: lightness, elevation, the "no crash" feeling

### Texture Guidelines
- Soft gradients over hard edges
- Organic shapes over geometric patterns
- Grain/noise is welcome — adds warmth and tactility
- Watercolor or ink-wash textures can complement cloud backgrounds

---

## Color Treatment in Photography

- Photos should lean warm — never cool/blue-toned
- Slight desaturation is fine, but never fully muted
- Highlights should feel creamy, not blown out
- Shadows should lean warm brown/navy, never pure black
- Post-processing should maintain the Cream (#FDF4EE) warmth of the brand
