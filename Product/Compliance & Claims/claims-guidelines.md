# shroome Claims & Compliance Guidelines

> Last updated: March 2026
> Owner: Product Lead / Legal
> Applies to: Website copy, email, social media, packaging, advertising

---

## Regulatory Framework

shroome is classified as a **dietary supplement** under the Dietary Supplement Health and Education Act (DSHEA) of 1994. This classification determines what claims we can and cannot make.

---

## Types of Claims

### Structure/Function Claims — ALLOWED

Structure/function claims describe the role of an ingredient in supporting the body's normal structure or function. These do **not** require FDA pre-approval but **do** require:

1. Truthfulness and substantiation (evidence the claim is not misleading)
2. The FDA disclaimer on all materials where claims appear
3. Notification to FDA within 30 days of first marketing (form FDA 3500)

**Approved structure/function claims for shroome:**

| Claim | Ingredient Basis |
|---|---|
| "Supports immune function" | Mushroom beta-glucans |
| "Supports sustained focus" | Matcha (caffeine + L-theanine) |
| "Supports healthy energy levels" | Matcha (caffeine) |
| "Supports skin health" | Collagen peptides |
| "Supports gut health" | Collagen peptides |
| "Provides antioxidant support" | Matcha (catechins, EGCG) |

### Disease Claims — PROHIBITED

Disease claims suggest that a product can diagnose, treat, cure, mitigate, or prevent a disease. These are **never allowed** for dietary supplements without FDA drug approval.

**Never say:**
- "Cures" or "treats" anything
- "Prevents [disease name]"
- "Reduces risk of [disease]"
- "Anti-cancer" or "anti-tumor"
- "Lowers cholesterol"
- "Boosts immunity" (implies therapeutic effect — use "supports immune function" instead)
- "Heals" anything
- "Clinically proven to..." (unless you have actual clinical trials on the finished product)

### Drug Claims — PROHIBITED

Any claim that positions the product as an alternative to medication is a drug claim.

**Never say:**
- "Better than Adderall for focus"
- "Natural alternative to [any drug]"
- "Replaces your morning medication"

## Required Disclaimers

### FDA Disclaimer (Required on all materials with structure/function claims)

> *These statements have not been evaluated by the Food and Drug Administration. This product is not intended to diagnose, treat, cure, or prevent any disease.*

**Placement rules:**
- Must appear on the product label (packaging)
- Must appear on the website product page
- Must appear in any advertising that includes structure/function claims
- Must be legible and conspicuous (not hidden in tiny footnotes)
- On social media: include in caption or as text overlay if making claims in the post

## Claim Language Guide

### Safe Phrasing

| Instead of... | Say... |
|---|---|
| "Boosts your immune system" | "Supports immune function" |
| "Gives you energy" | "Supports healthy energy levels" |
| "Improves focus" | "Supports sustained focus" |
| "Anti-aging" | "Supports skin health" |
| "Heals your gut" | "Supports gut health" |
| "Proven to work" | "Formulated with researched ingredients" |
| "Clinically proven" | "Ingredients studied for [function]" (only if true) |
| "Superfood" | Acceptable but use sparingly — it has no regulatory definition |

### Beta-Glucan Claims

Our 70%+ beta-glucan content is a key differentiator, but claims must stay within structure/function boundaries:

**Allowed:**
- "Contains 70%+ beta-glucan content from organic mushroom extracts"
- "Beta-glucans support immune function"
- "Higher beta-glucan concentration than typical mushroom supplements"

**Not allowed:**
- "Beta-glucans fight disease"
- "Beta-glucans cure [anything]"
- "Clinically proven beta-glucan formula" (unless we run our own clinical trial)

## Channel-Specific Guidelines

### Website (drinkshroome.com)
- FDA disclaimer on every page that contains claims
- Claims should be paired with ingredient references
- Avoid absolute statements ("the best," "the only," "guaranteed")

### Email
- FDA disclaimer in footer of any email containing product claims
- Education-focused emails can discuss ingredients and their studied functions
- Never promise specific health outcomes

### Social Media
- If a post makes a structure/function claim, include FDA disclaimer in the caption
- Influencer posts with claims must include both #ad/#partner AND the FDA disclaimer
- Comments and DMs: do not make claims in replies to customer questions about health outcomes

### Packaging
- FDA disclaimer on the label (Supplement Facts panel area)
- All structure/function claims on the package must be notified to FDA
- Ingredient list must match Supplement Facts exactly
- Net weight and serving size must be accurate

---

## Competitor Comparison Rules

- Never name competitors in claims comparisons
- "Higher beta-glucan content than typical mushroom supplements" is acceptable
- "Better than [Brand X]" is not acceptable
- Comparative claims must be substantiated with data
- Focus on what shroome IS rather than what competitors are NOT

---

## Testimonials & Reviews (Post-Launch)

- Customer testimonials cannot include disease claims even if the customer said it
- Edit or do not publish reviews that claim the product "cured" something
- Endorsements must reflect typical results, not exceptional outcomes
- FTC requires material connections (free product, payment) to be disclosed
