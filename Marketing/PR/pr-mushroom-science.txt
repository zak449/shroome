NEW FUNCTIONAL BEVERAGE BRAND SETS HIGHER STANDARD FOR MUSHROOM SUPPLEMENTS WITH 70%+ BETA-GLUCAN CONCENTRATION

Shroomé uses fruiting body-only extracts in its ceremonial matcha latte, challenging an industry where most products rely on mycelium grown on grain

UNITED STATES — Shroomé, maker of the world's first ready-to-pour ceremonial matcha latte, today called attention to a quality gap in the mushroom supplement industry. The company uses Lion's Mane and Reishi extracts sourced exclusively from fruiting bodies and standardized to more than 70 percent beta-glucan concentration — a threshold that most competing products fail to reach.

The distinction matters. Beta-glucans are the primary bioactive compounds responsible for the immune-supporting and cognitive benefits associated with functional mushrooms. However, the majority of mushroom supplements on the market today use mycelium cultivated on grain substrates. Independent testing has shown that mycelium-on-grain products can contain less than 5 percent beta-glucans, with a significant portion of the product composed of starch filler from the grain itself.

"Consumers are paying premium prices for mushroom supplements that are mostly rice starch," said Zachary Kaufman, founder of Shroomé. "The label says Lion's Mane. The lab results say grain. We think people deserve to know what they are actually consuming, and we built our formulation around that transparency."

Each Shroomé sachet contains 200 milligrams of mushroom extract — split between Lion's Mane and Reishi — derived solely from fruiting bodies. Lion's Mane has been studied for its potential to support nerve growth factor production and cognitive function. Reishi has a long history of use for immune modulation and stress adaptation. By using fruiting body extracts with verified beta-glucan content above 70 percent, Shroomé delivers a concentrated dose of these compounds without filler.

The company subjects its mushroom extracts to third-party testing to verify beta-glucan concentration, heavy metal levels and microbial safety. Certificates of analysis are available upon request.

"We are not anti-mycelium as a concept. Mycelium has legitimate applications in research," Kaufman said. "But when a consumer buys a functional mushroom product, they expect mushroom — not a grain product with trace amounts of fungal biomass. Standardizing to 70 percent beta-glucans is our way of setting a floor, not a ceiling."

Shroomé's matcha latte sachets pair these mushroom extracts with 2 grams of ceremonial grade matcha and 2 grams of grass-fed bovine collagen peptides in a ready-to-pour liquid format. The company is currently accepting waitlist signups at drinkshroome.com ahead of its first production run.
