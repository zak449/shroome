AS MATCHA MARKET SURGES PAST $4B, SHROOMÉ BETS ON CONVENIENCE TO WIN THE MORNING RITUAL

Ready-to-pour liquid sachets eliminate the preparation barrier that has kept ceremonial matcha out of daily routines

UNITED STATES — The global matcha market has surpassed $4 billion and is growing at a compound annual growth rate of 9.5 percent, fueled by consumer demand for sustained energy, L-theanine-driven focus and a cleaner alternative to coffee. But a persistent barrier stands between matcha and mass adoption: preparation. Shroomé, a new functional beverage company, is eliminating that friction entirely with the world's first ready-to-pour ceremonial matcha latte.

Traditional ceremonial matcha requires a bamboo whisk, a fine-mesh sifter, water heated to a precise temperature range and a technique that takes practice to master. Most consumers skip the process altogether, opting instead for lower-grade matcha powders that dissolve more easily but sacrifice the flavor and nutritional profile of true ceremonial grade. The result is a market where the highest-quality product is also the hardest to use.

"Matcha has a preparation problem," said Zachary Kaufman, founder of Shroomé. "The people who want the best matcha are stuck with a 5-minute ritual involving specialized tools. Everyone else settles for culinary grade powder mixed into a smoothie. We saw an opportunity to deliver ceremonial grade quality in a format that takes 15 seconds."

Shroomé's liquid sachets contain 2 grams of ceremonial grade matcha that is shade-grown for a minimum of 21 days and stone-ground in the traditional method. The matcha is pre-dissolved into a liquid concentrate, so users simply tear open a sachet, pour it into their preferred milk and stir. No whisking, no sifting, no clumps.

The format also allows Shroomé to add functional ingredients that would be difficult to incorporate into a dry powder without affecting dissolvability. Each sachet includes 200 milligrams of Lion's Mane and Reishi mushroom extracts from fruiting bodies, standardized to more than 70 percent beta-glucans, and 2 grams of grass-fed bovine collagen peptides. The product launches in two flavors: Vanilla Oat and Strawberry Rose.

"The matcha market is growing because consumers want what matcha delivers — calm energy, antioxidants, no crash," Kaufman said. "But growth will plateau if the category does not solve for convenience. We are making the case that you do not have to choose between quality and speed."

Shroomé is currently in pre-launch phase, with waitlist signups open at drinkshroome.com. The company plans to announce pricing and availability in the coming weeks.
