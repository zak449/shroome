# shroome Content Strategy Pillars

> Last updated: March 2026
> Owner: Marketing Director / Social Lead

---

## Overview

All shroome social content falls into one of four pillars. Every piece of content should map to a pillar before it gets made. If it doesn't fit, it doesn't ship.

The goal is not to go viral — it is to build a community of informed, loyal customers who understand what makes shroome different and feel good about telling their friends.

---

## Pillar 1: Product Education

**Purpose:** Teach people what functional ingredients actually do and why shroome's formulation matters.

**Key Topics:**
- Beta-glucans: what they are, why 70%+ concentration is rare, how they support immune function
- Mushroom extracts: the difference between mycelium-on-grain and real fruiting body extracts
- Ceremonial matcha: first-harvest, shade-grown, why grade matters for taste and caffeine quality
- Collagen peptides: grass-fed sourcing, bioavailability, why 2g is a meaningful daily dose
- Clean label: no fillers, no proprietary blends, every ingredient and dose listed

**Content Formats:**
- Carousel posts (ingredient deep-dives)
- Short-form video (30-60s explainers)
- Infographics comparing shroome to generic matcha or mushroom products
- "Did you know?" single-frame posts

**Tone:** Smart but accessible. Explain it like you would to a curious friend, not a biochemistry student.

**Frequency:** 2-3x per week

---

## Pillar 2: Lifestyle / Energy

**Purpose:** Show what daily life looks like with shroome — the energy, the ritual, the feeling.

**Key Topics:**
- Morning routines that don't require a cafe trip
- Sustained energy without the crash (vs. coffee, vs. energy drinks)
- The convenience of sachets — desk, travel, gym bag
- Taste profiles: Vanilla (warm, floral, grounding) and Strawberry (bright, fruity, fresh)
- "Cafe energy. Home address." — the core brand promise in action

**Content Formats:**
- POV/aesthetic reels (making the drink, morning light, first sip)
- User-generated content reposts (post-launch)
- Day-in-the-life content featuring the product naturally
- Before/after energy comparisons (lighthearted, not clinical)

**Tone:** Aspirational but real. This is not a luxury brand — it is an upgrade to what you already do every morning.

**Frequency:** 2-3x per week

**Image direction:** Warm lighting, real spaces, no whisks or traditional tea imagery. See `Brand/Photography & Image Direction/image-guidelines.md`.

---

## Pillar 3: Transparency

**Purpose:** Build trust by showing exactly what goes into the product and how decisions are made.

**Key Topics:**
- Full ingredient list with doses (nothing hidden behind "proprietary blend")
- Sourcing stories: where the matcha comes from, where the mushrooms are grown
- Why certain ingredients were chosen and others were left out
- Lab testing and quality standards
- Honest conversations about the supplement industry's problems (fairy-dusting, under-dosing, misleading labels)

**Content Formats:**
- Side-by-side label comparisons (shroome vs. the industry norm — no brand names)
- Behind-the-scenes of formulation decisions
- "Ask us anything" stories/Q&A sessions
- Short-form video: founder explaining a single ingredient decision

**Tone:** Confident, not defensive. We share because we are proud, not because we have something to prove.

**Frequency:** 1-2x per week

---

## Pillar 4: Community

**Purpose:** Make the waitlist and early customers feel like insiders, not subscribers.

**Key Topics:**
- Waitlist milestones and updates
- Founder story: why shroome exists, the personal journey
- Sneak peeks at packaging, product development, branding decisions
- Early supporter recognition
- Launch countdown and exclusive access

**Content Formats:**
- Story polls and questions (engage directly)
- Founder-to-camera videos (casual, unscripted)
- Milestone celebration posts (waitlist numbers, production updates)
- DM screenshots and community reactions (with permission)

**Tone:** Intimate, grateful, real. The founder is not a faceless CEO — they are the person making this happen.

**Frequency:** 1-2x per week

## Weekly Content Mix

| Day | Primary Pillar | Format Suggestion |
|---|---|---|
| Monday | Product Education | Carousel or infographic |
| Tuesday | Lifestyle / Energy | Reel or aesthetic post |
| Wednesday | Transparency | Behind-the-scenes or label breakdown |
| Thursday | Product Education | Short-form video explainer |
| Friday | Community | Founder update or milestone |
| Saturday | Lifestyle / Energy | Weekend morning ritual content |
| Sunday | Rest or Stories-only | Poll, Q&A, or casual story |

---

## Platform Priority

1. **Instagram** — Primary channel. Feed + Stories + Reels.
2. **TikTok** — Secondary. Repurpose Reels, test education content.
3. **Email** — Owned channel. Drives deepest engagement. See `Marketing/Email/email-strategy.md`.
4. **Twitter/X** — Low priority pre-launch. Founder voice only.

---

## Content Guardrails

- Never make disease claims or medical promises (see `Product/Compliance & Claims/claims-guidelines.md`)
- Never use competitor brand names in posts
- Never use stock photography — real product and real people only
- Never use urgency/scarcity language for waitlist or discounts
- All product claims must be structure/function claims with appropriate disclaimers
- Influencer content must be FTC-compliant with clear #ad or #partner disclosure
